The Measured Database of Canadian Index Funds

This database keeps track of the vast majority of Canadian index funds. Key metrics such as total assets under management (AUM), management expense ratios (MER), and performance / yield data are included when available.

 

 

Index Fund Providers

As it stands, this database includes funds from 6 of the top providers in Canada, as shown in the chart above. According to statistics from the Canadian ETF Association, there are a total of 33 fund providers in Canada, with total AUM of $156.8B. As such, this database covers ~87% of the total Canadian market in terms of AUM.

Given the manual work required to pull data from the long tail of index fund providers, the remaining 27 providers have been excluded from this analysis. I'll do my best to include more fund providers in future updates to this database.

 

Asset Class Summary

The lion's share of assets are invested in equity funds (69%) and fixed income funds (30%).

While 'Asset Allocation' funds (which invest in a mix and equity and fixed income assets, with automatic-rebalancing) only manage $1B of assets today, I'd expect this number to grow significantly in the coming years. These funds offer investors an easy path to sticking with a target asset allocation without needing to manually calculate weightings and execute additional trades.

Funds which primarily invest in commodities, alternative assets (e.g., hedge funds), and currency are just a brief blip on the screen.

Management Expense Ratio (MER) Distribution

Turning to the annual fees charged by Canadian index funds, we can see a bi-modal distribution. There's a clump of funds charging between 0.2%-0.3%, and another large group of funds charging 0.6%-0.7%.

Here's hoping that the fees continue to drop, following the lead of our neighbour to the south, where funds with zero management fees have recently been introduced.

A few technical notes:

  • AUM, performance, and yield data is as of Dec. 31, 2018
  • All performance data represents the total return of each fund, net of fees. This means that dividends are assumed to be re-invested back into the fund, and that the management fees have been subtracted
  • The yield percentages shown represent the distributions from the funds over the past 12 months. However, for Invesco funds, the yield % represents the implied annualized yield from the previous month's distribution (as opposed to an entire 12 month period). As such, the yield figures for Invesco funds should be treated as directional at best, given that the distribution for a fund in a single month can diverge significantly from a typical annual average
